On Friendship in Your 20s

2026-04-17

Last year wasn’t the greatest for me. I was challenged with emotionally charged decisions for what felt like every single part of the year. Being somewhat of a Type A and a planner, each of the challenges made me question my being and how i approached my personal life. I faced some of these alone, but a large chunk of them with my friends.

Friendships go through an interesting change after graduating college. Effort in friendships becomes much more apparent as time goes on, and it was something I felt quite a bit. I don’t think it’s even about misplaced trust, but maybe having some hopefulness. People you’ve grown up beside, faced impossible tasks, had conversations with past 3am and sometimes they fade away.

Friendship to me has changed forms in different stages of my life. Middle school and high school were rougher - where I made “friends” who’d cozy up to me when they needed help with math or science. University changed it again where I thought having everyone around me was a friend of mine and surrounding myself with as many people would bring safety and peace; not having had that in my life prior. And then post grad has been shedding some of these notions and even figuring out what it really means to have a friend. I think now Id say I know a lot of people, but they aren’t my friends.

Coming to this was excruciatingly difficult. Not only coming to terms with the fact that our relationship wasn’t going to be the same, but also shedding the belief that I needed people around me always to feel okay. Some of us grew apart and some previous friends of mine just don’t see eye-to-eye anymore. To me - there must be some energy exchanged for friendship. It can be shown in different ways like planning a hangout, initiation a conversation, or being there for your friends when they’re going through it.

Since i had gotten used to having many friends around me, peeling back the layers of who is actually important and has my back is tough to do when you’re attached to the person and what you’ve shared for so long. You go through the 5 stages of grief much like a breakup and similarly come out with a better sense of who you are.

your 20s are a time why things move fast for some, slow for others, but chaotic in its own little ways for everyone. finding people who you trust and have your back is one is the greatest things you can have. i hope these friendships stay alive for the top is lonely without friends.
